Debate Summarization Evaluation

Please do not complete this task, Summary Comparison 2, more than once.


The online world is full of debate and discussion - could these online debates be summarized automatically? We are investigating this question for an undergraduate research project and are asking people to compare different summaries. Making an effort to remain indifferent to the topics, please complete the form below to let us know what you think.

Please contact c.egan.12@aberdeen.ac.uk if you have any queries before starting the survey.

Instructions

We will ask you to compare two summaries side-by-side, that differ in content or presentation, of the same online debate.

The task is just about the comparison of summaries at face value since reading the long source text would take too long.

Please remember that this is a student research project and that summaries are not intended to give preference to a particular viewpoint, nor should you allow your personal stance on a topic influence your decision.

In the following sections you will be asked to compare summaries on the following factors:

  • Content Interest / Informativeness the summary presents varied and interesting content.
  • Readability the summary contents make sense; work without context; aren't repetitive; and are easy to read.
  • Punctuation and Presentation the summary contents are correctly formatted as sentences, punctuation, capital letters and have sentence case.
  • Organization related points occur near one and other.
  • Overall aggregating your scores from those listed above - and any other factors you deem relevant - select your preferred summary.

Please also note that lines in summaries are to be read independently (they are not paragraphs).

A summary is not intended to present a structured argument on the topic but rather give you a feel for what people talk about.
